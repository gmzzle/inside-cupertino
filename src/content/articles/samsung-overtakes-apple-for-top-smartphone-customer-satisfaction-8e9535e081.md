[MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/05/20/samsung-overtakes-apple-for-customer-satisfaction/) reported that Samsung now leads Apple 81 to 80 in the American Customer Satisfaction Index's annual smartphone study. It's the first time in recent memory Apple hasn't tied or topped the category, and while a one-point gap is basically a rounding error, the optics aren't great.

The report credits Samsung's win to features that deliver "everyday value without introducing new pain points" - which sounds like a polite way of saying Apple's recent moves haven't all landed well. Battery life improvements across the industry rose 5% this year, which helps everyone, but Apple's been burned before by overpromising AI features that drain batteries faster than users expect. iOS 18's intelligence rollout was bumpy enough that some users turned features off entirely just to get through a workday.

What's interesting is that AI features scored 85 overall in customer satisfaction, nearly matching the 86 that phone calls and texting get. That's shockingly high for technology that's barely been in consumers' hands for two years. It suggests people actually use and value the AI stuff, which means Apple's slow, cautious approach to AI integration might be costing them more than just satisfaction points. Samsung's been more aggressive about shipping Galaxy AI features, even if they're imperfect, and customers seem fine with that tradeoff.

Apple's satisfaction score has been stuck in the low 80s for years now, which isn't bad but also isn't the runaway leadership they enjoyed a decade ago. The iPhone has become a mature product, and mature products live or die on execution details: battery life, reliability, whether the new features actually work as advertised. Apple's usually great at this, but they've had enough software stumbles lately - notification summaries getting facts wrong, Siri still being Siri - that a one-point slide feels earned.

Samsung's victory lap will be short-lived if the next iPhone nails the basics, but right now Apple's playing catch-up in a category they used to own.
