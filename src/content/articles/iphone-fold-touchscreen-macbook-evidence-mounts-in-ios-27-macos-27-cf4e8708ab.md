Apple just leaked its own hardware again. [AppleInsider](https://appleinsider.com/articles/26/06/14/iphone-fold-touchscreen-macbook-evidence-mounts-in-ios-27-macos-27?utm_source=rss) caught references in the iOS 27 and macOS 27 Golden Gate betas that point to both a folding iPhone and a MacBook with a touchscreen—two products Apple has publicly waffled on for years while Samsung and Microsoft have shipped theirs.

The timing makes sense if you've been watching the pattern. Apple typically seeds references to unreleased hardware about 12-18 months before launch, often in accessibility frameworks or display management code. They did it with the notch before iPhone X, with ProMotion before the 2021 MacBook Pros, and with the Dynamic Island before iPhone 14 Pro. This isn't accidental—it's infrastructure prep. Developers need time to test edge cases, and Apple needs time to refine the APIs without announcing anything concrete. The difference this time is how explicit the references apparently are, which suggests either these products are closer than we thought or Apple's testing the public reaction.

The touchscreen Mac is the less surprising piece. Apple killed the Touch Bar, but they didn't kill the underlying desire for direct manipulation on laptop screens—they just needed the right implementation. A MacBook Pro with an OLED touchscreen running macOS 27 would address the ergonomic complaints (no arm fatigue from reaching up constantly) while giving pros the occasional direct-select convenience for things like color pickers and timeline scrubbing. It's not iPad mode bolted onto a laptop; it's a Mac that happens to let you touch the screen when it's faster than the trackpad.

The foldable iPhone is messier. Samsung's on its sixth generation of the Galaxy Z Fold, and Apple's still figuring out the hinge. That's either extreme caution or a sign that the tech still doesn't meet their durability standards. A foldable iPhone would need to survive years of daily folding without the crease turning into a canyon, and current materials aren't quite there yet. But if iOS 27 is laying groundwork, we're probably looking at a 2027 or 2028 launch window—just enough time for screen tech to improve and for Apple to charge $2,000 for it.

This is Apple finally admitting that form factors they publicly dismissed are inevitable. Took them long enough.
