[AppleInsider](https://appleinsider.com/articles/26/07/07/iphone-18-pros-camera-bump-could-be-a-little-bit-thicker?utm_source=rss) is running a Weibo rumor that the iPhone 18 Pro's camera bump will grow by about 2mm compared to the iPhone 17 Pro. That's a lot for a component Apple usually tries to shave down, not bulk up.

The obvious read: Apple's hit the limits of what it can do with the current camera plateau design. The iPhone 17 Pro measures 8.75mm thick, and the camera sensors inside are already pushing against physics—larger sensors need more physical depth for light capture, and computational photography can only compensate so much. If Apple wants better low-light performance or tighter zoom without sacrificing image quality, the glass and sensor stack needs room to breathe. A 2mm bump isn't Apple giving up on thinness; it's Apple prioritizing the thing people actually use their phones for.

This also tracks with the broader industry trend. Samsung's Galaxy S25 Ultra and Google's Pixel 10 Pro both added camera module thickness in the past year, and neither company got pilloried for it. Phones stopped getting meaningfully thinner around 2020 because battery life and thermal management matter more than shaving another millimeter off the chassis. The camera bump has become the place where practicality wins.

The timing is interesting too. Apple's rumored periscope zoom upgrade for the iPhone 18 Pro would require more internal space, and the company's been testing new sensor coatings that perform better with thicker lens assemblies. A 2mm bump could accommodate both without requiring a full chassis redesign. It's an aluminum frame tweak, not a ground-up rethink, which makes this rumor feel more like a leak than speculation.

If this holds, expect Apple to spin it as a feature—"our most advanced camera system yet" or some variation. And honestly, they wouldn't be wrong. Nobody's going to miss 2mm of thinness if the photos look better.
