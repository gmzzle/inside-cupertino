Apple released a trailer for its next Apple Immersive Video on Wednesday. Where did they put it? YouTube. In 2D. Not in the Vision Pro app where people who actually own the $3,500 headset might see it. Not even a notification inside visionOS. Just a standard YouTube upload that could've been promoting literally anything else.

[AppleInsider](https://appleinsider.com/articles/26/05/20/latest-apple-immersive-rollout-exemplifies-apple-vision-pros-entire-problem?utm_source=rss) called this "Apple Vision Pro's entire problem," and it's hard to argue. Immersive Video is one of the headset's few genuinely exclusive features - the stuff you literally cannot experience anywhere else. It's the kind of content that should make Vision Pro owners feel like they bought into something special. Instead, Apple markets it to people who don't own the device and can't even watch it properly.

This isn't a one-off. Apple has been weirdly quiet about Vision Pro since launch. No stage time at recent events. Minimal software updates. The spatial video recording feature they pushed hard for iPhone 15 Pro? Great for creating content, but there's still barely any professional content to watch. The handful of Immersive Videos we've gotten - Alicia Keys, prehistoric animals, a few sports things - dropped with little fanfare and zero sustained marketing push.

Compare this to how Apple treated Apple TV+ in its early days, or even how Meta promotes Quest exclusives. Those platforms got relentless promotion, developer showcases, launch events. Vision Pro feels like Apple built something technically brilliant, released it, then moved on to the next thing before bothering to feed it.

The hardware is still the best spatial computer you can buy. The software foundation is solid. But platforms don't survive on potential - they survive on momentum, and momentum requires Apple to act like Vision Pro matters. A 2D YouTube trailer isn't it.
