Apple raised prices across the MacBook and iPad lines earlier this week, and now Amazon is scrambling to move inventory before the new MSRPs take effect. [9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/06/25/prime-day-deals-macbook-air-macbook-neo-airpods-pro-3/) reports the M5 MacBook Air is $350 off and the M5 Pro/Max models are getting slashed by up to $800, which sounds great until you realize these discounts are measured against prices that didn't exist three days ago.

The base MacBook Neo is $110 off, AirPods Pro 3 are down $70, and AirPods Max 2 are $150 off but nearly sold out. The new AirTag 2 is also hitting all-time lows, which makes sense given it launched barely two months ago and Apple priced it like a luxury good. Amazon's clearly trying to clear out stock before the new price floor makes these deals look less aggressive on paper.

This is the retail equivalent of a hostage negotiation. Apple unilaterally raises prices, Amazon eats margin to keep moving units, and consumers are left doing mental math to figure out if they're actually saving money or just paying what these products cost last month. The M5 Air at $350 off is legitimately a good price, but that discount only exists because Apple added $200 to the base MSRP on Tuesday. It's a sale against an artificial ceiling.

The MacBook Neo discounts are more interesting because that product is still finding its audience. Knocking $110 off a $999 entry-level Mac is Amazon saying they're not confident it'll move at full price, especially now that the Air starts at $1,199. The Neo was supposed to be Apple's affordability play, but it's already getting the Costco treatment.

If you're in the market for an M5 Mac or new AirPods, today's actually a decent time to buy - just don't mistake Amazon's desperation for generosity.
