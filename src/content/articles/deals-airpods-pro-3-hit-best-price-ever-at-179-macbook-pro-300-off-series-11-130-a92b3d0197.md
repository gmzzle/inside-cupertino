AirPods Pro 3 are down to $179 at Amazon, according to [9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/06/10/deals-airpods-pro-240w-beats-cable-macbook-pro/)—nearly 30% off a product that launched in September 2025. That's the kind of discount you see when a retailer knows something you don't.

Apple doesn't usually let its flagship audio product crater in price this fast unless there's a replacement coming. The AirPods Pro 2 held their value for over two years before discounts got aggressive. The Pro 3 haven't even hit their first birthday and they're already getting blown out. Either demand fell off a cliff (unlikely, given how well they reviewed), or Apple told its retail partners to clear inventory. My money's on AirPods Pro 4 showing up at the fall event.

The rest of the deals are more predictable. M5 MacBook Pros are $300 off because the M6 models are probably six weeks away, and Apple Watch Series 11 is discounted because it's June and the Series 12 launches in September like clockwork. The 15-inch M5 MacBook Air at $200 off is a solid buy if you don't need the latest chip—the Air's design cycle moves slower than the Pro, so you're not getting burned as badly by waiting.

What's weird is the Beats USB-C cable showing up in an Apple deals roundup. It's a 240W braided cable for $19, which is fine, but it's also the kind of accessory that signals Apple's trying to move everything in the ecosystem. You've got USB-C on iPhones, iPads, AirPods cases, and now even the Magic Mouse if you bought the new one. The cable being prominently discounted feels less like a deal and more like Apple making sure you have the right gear for the next wave of products.

If you need AirPods Pro right now, $179 is as good as it's going to get. Just know you're buying the outgoing model, and the new ones will probably have better noise cancellation, longer battery life, or some health sensor Apple's been working on. That's the trade-off.
