[AppleInsider](https://appleinsider.com/articles/26/05/31/slimmer-lighter-apple-vision-pro-is-at-least-two-years-away?utm_source=rss) reports the next Vision Pro is now slated for 2028 or 2029, which means we're looking at a four- or five-year gap between Gen 1 and Gen 2. For context, that's longer than Apple typically waits between major product revisions. The iPhone goes annual. The iPad used to be annual before settling into a more relaxed cadence. But Vision Pro isn't a phone or a tablet, and treating it like one would be a mistake.

The delay makes sense because Apple doesn't yet know what this product needs to be. The current Vision Pro is a technical marvel that almost nobody bought. It's too heavy, too expensive, and solving for problems most people don't have. Iterating quickly on that formula just gets you a slightly better version of a thing the market already rejected. What Apple needs is time to figure out whether the next move is a cheaper headset, a lighter one, or something that doesn't sit on your face at all—like the smart glasses they're also reportedly working on.

A 2028 ship date also gives component suppliers time to catch up. The displays, chips, and batteries required to make a meaningfully thinner and lighter Vision Pro at a meaningfully lower price point don't exist yet, at least not in a form Apple would ship. Waiting lets the supply chain mature, costs come down, and Apple avoid the trap of launching Vision Pro 1.5 when what people actually want is Vision Pro 2.0.

The real question is whether Apple can keep the product line alive in the meantime. Four years is a long time to sell a $3,500 headset that most developers have already written off. If the current Vision Pro becomes a zombie product—technically available but effectively abandoned—it poisons the well for whatever comes next. Apple's banking on patience, which is fine, but only if they're also banking on having something genuinely different to show for it.
