[9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/05/23/belkin-5000mah-magsafe-slim-battery-bank-hands-on-kickstand/) got hands-on with Belkin's new 5,000mAh MagSafe battery bank, and the headline feature is simple: it has a kickstand. Which sounds boring until you remember that Apple's own MagSafe Battery Pack never had one, and that omission was baffling from day one.

Belkin's version is slim enough to not feel like you're carrying a brick, and the kickstand folds flat when you're not using it. The 5,000mAh capacity won't fully charge a modern iPhone, but it'll get you through a long day if you're starting from 40-50%. That's the same capacity as Apple's discontinued pack, which cost $99 and couldn't prop your phone up. Belkin's pricing isn't confirmed in the hands-on, but their previous MagSafe batteries ran $40-60, which makes this a no-brainer if it lands in that range.

The kickstand angle matters more than it sounds like it should. MagSafe charging is already slow compared to a cable, so you're usually letting the phone sit there for a while. Being able to prop it up for FaceTime, or to watch something while it charges, turns a charging accessory into something you actually want to use. Apple understood this with the MagSafe Duo and various charging stands, but never connected the dots on the battery pack.

Belkin also makes a Qi2 version of this battery for non-iPhone devices, which is the same hardware minus the Apple tax. Qi2 is basically MagSafe for Android, and it's finally starting to show up in real products two years after the spec was announced. If you've got a Qi2 Android phone, this is one of the first accessories worth buying.

Apple killed its MagSafe Battery Pack in 2024 and never replaced it. Belkin just did.
