[9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/06/19/deals-iphone-17-pro-airpods-apple-watch-macbook-pro/) rounded up today's Apple deals, and the headline number jumps out: iPhone 17 Pro Max discounted up to $250, the regular Pro up to $290 off. We're talking about phones that launched in February. Four months ago. That's not normal.

Apple's flagship iPhones don't typically see this kind of markdown until they're about to be replaced or demand has completely flatlined. The iPhone 16 Pro hung around full price for most of its cycle. The 15 Pro did too. When you see $290 off a current-gen Pro model in June, it means one of two things: retailers ordered too many and need to clear inventory, or customers aren't buying at the pace Apple projected. Neither scenario is great for Cupertino.

The rest of the deals are more predictable. AirPods at $99 is solid if you've been waiting. The M5 Pro MacBook Pro at $200 off matches its all-time low, which makes sense given the M6 chip is probably three months out. Apple Watch Ultra 3 at nearly $100 off is rare but not shocking for a product that's been out since September. Standard retail cycle stuff.

But those iPhone 17 Pro discounts land differently. Apple spent the spring positioning the 17 lineup as a major leap - thinner bezels, the new A19 chip, improved thermals, all that. If the pitch worked, you wouldn't see this kind of price erosion this fast. Maybe the $1,199 starting price for the Pro was too aggressive. Maybe people are waiting for the rumored foldable next year. Maybe the incremental improvements just weren't enough to pull upgraders off their 15 Pros.

Whatever the reason, when your four-month-old flagship is getting the clearance treatment, the market is telling you something you probably don't want to hear.
