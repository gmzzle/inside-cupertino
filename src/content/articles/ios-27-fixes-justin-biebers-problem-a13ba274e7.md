Apple shipped iOS 27 with a relocated dictation button in Messages, and it's hard not to notice the timing. Last year Justin Bieber posted about how annoying the mic button placement was—apparently he kept triggering it when he didn't mean to—and now it's moved. [MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/06/09/ios-27-fixes-justin-bieber-problem/) flagged the change, and yeah, it does look like Apple listened to a pop star's Instagram story.

This isn't the first time a high-profile complaint has seemingly nudged Apple into action, but it's one of the more straightforward examples. The old placement sat right next to the text input field, which made accidental taps common if you were typing quickly or had larger hands. Bieber's gripe was that he'd constantly hit dictation mid-sentence, interrupt his flow, and have to cancel out. It's a minor annoyance that turns major when it happens three times in one conversation.

What's interesting is that this wasn't a widespread complaint in Apple's feedback channels—or at least, not one that bubbled up visibly. There were occasional Reddit threads and a few tweets here and there, but nothing close to the volume of, say, complaints about autocorrect or notification management. Bieber's post got millions of impressions. Apple's design team probably saw it before lunch.

The new placement in iOS 27 moves the dictation button further from the text field and closer to the keyboard switcher. It's a sensible tweak, the kind of thing that probably should have happened years ago but didn't because no one with enough reach made noise about it. Whether Apple would have made this change without the celebrity signal boost is unknowable, but the fact that it happened a year later—exactly the timeline for an iOS point update—suggests someone at least put it on the list.

Sometimes good UI changes come from user research. Sometimes they come from Justin Bieber getting annoyed enough to post about it.
