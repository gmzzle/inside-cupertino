[9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/07/22/ted-lasso-season-four-has-season-one-magic-per-report/) is passing along word from The Hollywood Reporter that Ted Lasso season four has recaptured "season one magic," which is exactly what you'd expect Apple to be whispering to entertainment reporters right now.

The third season was a mess. It dragged, it lost the plot, and it made a lot of people wonder if the show had simply run its course after the near-perfect arc of seasons one and two. Apple needs this to work—Ted Lasso isn't just a show for them, it's the show. It's the one title casual viewers can actually name when you ask what's on Apple TV+. Losing that would be a significant blow to a service that still struggles with awareness despite throwing billions at content.

The question is whether anyone still cares. Season three aired in 2023, and we're now looking at a 2026 or 2027 premiere for season four. That's a long gap for a comedy that thrived on momentum and warmth. The streaming landscape has moved on. Audiences have moved on. Apple bet big on bringing back Jason Sudeikis, Hannah Waddingham, and Brett Goldstein, but they're also betting that goodwill from 2020 hasn't completely evaporated.

If the new season really does recapture what made the first one work—the tightness, the heart, the lack of bloat—it could be a win. Apple would get to claim they stuck the landing, and Ted Lasso could go out on a high note instead of the sour one season three left. But early positive whispers are standard operating procedure for a flagship show. The real test is whether viewers show up, and whether they stay for more than the first episode out of nostalgia.

Apple can't afford another stumble here, and they know it.
