[9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/05/31/new-apple-tv-and-homepod-mini-launching-this-fall-apple-intelligence/) reports that Apple's planning updated Apple TV and HomePod mini units for this fall, timed to the launch of next-generation Siri in iOS 27. Both are essentially chip bumps, not redesigns. The Apple TV 4K might also get a tweaked Siri Remote.

This is less exciting than it sounds, but it makes sense. The current Apple TV 4K runs on the A15 from 2021. The HomePod mini uses the ancient S5 chip from the Apple Watch Series 5. If Apple's serious about running on-device Apple Intelligence features through Siri—especially the contextual, multi-step stuff promised in iOS 27—both boxes need more horsepower. You can't process natural language queries locally on a chip from 2019.

The Siri Remote refresh is the wild card here. Apple already fixed the worst remote in consumer electronics back in 2021 with the silver aluminum version that ditched the glass trackpad. What's left to improve? Maybe USB-C instead of Lightning, now that the EU mandate has forced Apple's hand everywhere else. Maybe Find My integration so you can actually locate the thing when it slides between couch cushions. A full redesign seems unlikely—Apple doesn't update accessories that often, and the current one works fine.

What's interesting is the timing. Apple's living room hardware has been on autopilot for years. The last real Apple TV update was 2022. The HomePod mini hasn't changed since launch in 2020. Refreshing both at once suggests Apple sees the home as the next frontier for Apple Intelligence, not just phones and Macs. Makes sense—voice assistants actually belong in stationary speakers and TV boxes, not just your pocket.

Still, chip upgrades don't move units. Unless next-gen Siri is meaningfully better than what we have now, most people won't notice the difference between an A15 and whatever Apple ships this fall.
