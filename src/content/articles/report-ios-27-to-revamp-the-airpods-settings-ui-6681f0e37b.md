[9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/05/24/report-ios-27-to-revamp-the-airpods-settings-ui/) reported that iOS 27 will bring a redesigned AirPods settings interface, supposedly "more functional, better organized and more streamlined." About time.

The current AirPods settings page is genuinely bad. It's a vertical scroll of toggles and submenus with no clear hierarchy—noise cancellation settings mixed with Find My options mixed with firmware details. If you want to adjust something specific, you're hunting. Compare that to how Apple treats Apple Watch in the dedicated Watch app, where features are grouped logically and there's actual breathing room. AirPods get treated like a Bluetooth speaker.

What's interesting is that Apple's apparently not going the standalone app route. The Apple Watch got its own app back in 2015 because it needed pairing flows and complications management that didn't fit in Settings. HomePod got the Home app. But AirPods, despite being a massive product line with multiple SKUs and increasingly complex features—Adaptive Audio, Conversation Awareness, head tracking, hearing health—still live buried in Bluetooth settings like it's 2016. A redesign within Settings suggests Apple thinks the problem is layout, not location. Maybe they're right. The AirPods experience is supposed to be frictionless and automatic. A dedicated app might overcomplicate what should be simple.

Still, this redesign needs to solve real problems. Spatial audio settings are currently a mess across AirPods Pro, AirPods Max, and regular AirPods. Firmware updates happen silently with no clear status indicator. And good luck explaining to someone where to find the ear tip fit test. If iOS 27's version is just a reshuffled list of the same toggles, it's a missed opportunity.

The real test will be whether Apple can make advanced features discoverable without making basic functionality harder to reach. That's the balance the Watch app mostly gets right and the current AirPods page completely whiffs.
