[MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/05/29/apple-wallet-ids-coming-to-15th-state/) reports Virginia is getting Wallet-based driver's licenses soon, making it the 15th state to support the feature. That's counting Puerto Rico in the total, which is a bit of creative scorekeeping since it's a territory, but sure. Arkansas just came online this week.

Here's the thing: Apple announced this feature in September 2021. We're now halfway through 2026. Fifteen states in nearly five years is not the kind of adoption curve that transforms how people carry ID. For context, Apple Pay launched in 2014 and was accepted at over a million U.S. locations within two years. This? This is the opposite of that.

The problem isn't Apple. The tech works fine. TSA accepts Wallet IDs at security checkpoints in participating states, a handful of apps check age with it, and the setup flow is straightforward. The problem is state DMVs, which move at the speed of government IT procurement. Each state has to build its own backend, navigate privacy regulations, and presumably convince someone in the statehouse that this isn't a plot to microchip the population. It's exhausting and slow.

Virginia's DMV said months ago this was coming "soon," which in government-speak could mean next week or next fiscal year. When it does go live, Virginians will tap the plus sign in Wallet, pick their state, scan their physical license, and take a selfie. The whole thing takes maybe five minutes. Then they'll have a digital ID they can use at... airport security, mostly. Maybe a bar that's feeling ambitious.

The real test isn't how many states have this - it's whether you can ditch your physical wallet entirely, and the answer is still no for most of the country.
