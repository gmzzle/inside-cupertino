Apple's July price adjustments are creating a weird discount window. [9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/07/01/deals-airtag-2-m4-ipad-air-magic-keyboard-satechi/) spotted M4 iPad Air models at $350 off and M5 iPad Pros down $300 at Amazon—but only on remaining stock. Once those units ship, the new pricing kicks in and these deals evaporate.

The Magic Keyboard situation is more interesting. The full-size model with Touch ID and numpad hit an all-time low at Amazon, and the basic Magic Keyboard is down to $80. Apple doesn't discount its own keyboards this aggressively, which suggests retailers are motivated to move inventory before something changes—either new models or adjusted wholesale pricing that kills their margins. Either way, if you've been waiting on a Magic Keyboard, this is probably the moment.

The AirTag 2 four-pack being cheaper than the original AirTag ever was tells you everything about Apple's pricing strategy evolution. They launched AirTag at $99 for four, clearly leaving room to move down. AirTag 2 has better range and precision finding, but Apple priced it to undercut the aftermarket that had sprung up around AirTag 1. Smart move—kill the knockoffs by making your own product the value play.

Satechi's deals are less about Apple's pricing and more about Prime Day hangover. Their Mac mini hub and iPhone accessories are back at Prime Day levels, which means they're probably clearing room for new SKUs. The $40 Magic Keyboard clone is Satechi's usual play: look enough like Apple's design that people shopping by image might grab it, but price it low enough that the quality gap doesn't sting.

If you're in the market for any of this stuff, buy it this week. Amazon's model is to pull discounts the instant they stop moving units, and once Apple's price changes take effect, the algorithm will reset everything to match the new MSRP baseline.
