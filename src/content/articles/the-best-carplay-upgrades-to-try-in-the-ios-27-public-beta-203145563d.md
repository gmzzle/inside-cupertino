[9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/07/17/the-best-carplay-upgrades-to-try-in-the-ios-27-public-beta/) rounded up the CarPlay changes in iOS 27's public beta, and honestly, it's the first time in years the car experience feels like more than an afterthought. Apple's been promising a CarPlay revolution since WWDC 2022, when they teased deep system integration with automakers. That's still vaporware. But iOS 27 delivers actual usable stuff right now.

The standout is multi-stop routing in Apple Maps, which should've existed a decade ago but didn't. You can finally plan a road trip with charging stops, bathroom breaks, and lunch without opening your phone or using Google Maps like a reasonable person. Siri also got less stupid in the car—it can now parse "play that song from last week" without making you spell out the track title like you're talking to a particularly dim voice assistant from 2015. The Now Playing interface got a redesign that's cleaner and shows more metadata at a glance, which matters when you're trying to skip a track at 70 mph.

What's quietly interesting is the focus on refining what CarPlay already does rather than chasing the next-gen integration that automakers keep slow-walking. Apple seems to have figured out that most people just want their phone's apps on a bigger screen, reliably, without the car's native UI fighting them. GM tried to kill CarPlay entirely and got slapped down by customer backlash. Rivian and Tesla still refuse to support it, which is fine if you love their software, less fine if you don't.

The public beta timing is smart, too—this drops mid-summer, so people have months to test it before fall road trips. Apple's gotten better about CarPlay beta stability in recent years. iOS 25's beta was a disaster that randomly disconnected mid-drive, but iOS 26 was solid by public beta 2.

CarPlay finally feels like a product team that's allowed to ship incremental wins instead of waiting for the Big Vision to materialize.
