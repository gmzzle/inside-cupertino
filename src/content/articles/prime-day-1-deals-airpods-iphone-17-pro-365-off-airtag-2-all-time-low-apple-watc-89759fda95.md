[9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/06/23/prime-day-deals-airpods-iphone-17-airtag-2-apple-watch/) reports Amazon is running Prime Day discounts across Apple's lineup, with some genuinely notable pricing. The $365 off iPhone 17 Pro sounds great until you notice the fine print: it's Amazon Renewed Premium. That's their refurb program, which is fine if you're comfortable with it, but calling it a straight "Prime Day discount" is doing some heavy lifting. It's a deal, sure. It's just not the same as $365 off new.

The more interesting story is AirTag 2 hitting an all-time low already. Apple launched the second-gen tracker in April, so we're talking about a product that's barely two months old getting its first real discount. That's faster than usual for Apple accessories, and it suggests either Amazon ordered heavy or initial demand wasn't quite what Cupertino hoped. Either way, if you've been holding off on the updated AirTag with its better range and precision finding, this is probably your window.

The AirPods Max 2 at $150 off is legitimacy steep for a product Apple just refreshed in March with USB-C and improved noise cancellation. Same for the $70 discount on AirPods Pro 3, which only launched in February with the new H3 chip. Apple rarely discounts current-gen audio gear this aggressively outside of Black Friday, so Amazon is clearly using Apple's premium products as loss leaders to drive Prime subscriptions and keep people in the ecosystem during summer—historically a dead zone for consumer electronics sales.

The black Magic Mouse discount is amusing mostly because that thing is still using a Lightning port on the bottom in 2026, and Apple apparently has no plans to fix either the charging situation or the ergonomics that have been a running joke since 2015. But sure, $15 off makes it slightly less of a bad purchase.

Bottom line: if you're already planning to buy Apple stuff and you have Prime, this is a solid moment to pull the trigger on accessories and wearables. Just read the fine print on that iPhone deal before you get excited.
