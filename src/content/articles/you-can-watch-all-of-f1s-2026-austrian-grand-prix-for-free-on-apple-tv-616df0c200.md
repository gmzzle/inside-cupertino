[MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/06/18/f1-2026-austrian-grand-prix-free-on-apple-tv/) reported that Apple's making the entire Austrian Grand Prix weekend free to stream on Apple TV+ from June 26-28. Practice, qualifying, sprint, the whole thing. No subscription required.

This is the clearest sign yet that F1 viewership on Apple TV+ isn't where Apple hoped it would be. Free preview weekends are what you do when conversion isn't working. Apple paid a reported $2 billion for global F1 rights starting in 2025, betting that Liberty Media's Netflix-fueled momentum would translate to streaming subscriptions. Eighteen months in, they're apparently still trying to convince people the product exists.

The Austrian Grand Prix makes sense as the sample platter. It's at Red Bull's home track, it's a sprint weekend so there's more content to hook people, and it falls right before the summer break when casual fans might be thinking about how to follow the second half of the season. But offering every session for free is more generous than the usual "watch qualifying, pay for the race" approach. That suggests Apple's really hunting for eyeballs.

The timing also matters because this is when Apple typically runs its back-to-school promotions. Giving away a marquee sports weekend pairs nicely with hardware sales and the "three months free" offers that hit in July. If you can get someone to watch Austria for free, maybe they'll bite on a discounted annual plan when the promotion ends. It's Marketing 101, but it only makes sense if your current subscriber base is too small to matter.

Apple's still not breaking out F1 viewership numbers, which tells you everything. They shout about Ted Lasso and Severance ratings when they're good. Sports rights are supposed to be the subscription anchor that keeps people paying year-round, but if you're giving away entire race weekends eighteen months into a ten-year deal, the anchor isn't holding.
