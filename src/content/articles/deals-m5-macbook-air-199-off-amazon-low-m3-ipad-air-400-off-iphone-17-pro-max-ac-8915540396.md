Amazon's dropping the 15-inch M5 MacBook Air by $199 to a new low, and [9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/05/21/deals-m5-macbook-air-m3-ipad-air-iphone-17-pro-max/) is calling it an "all-time low" like that's a selling point. But here's the thing: the M5 Air only launched in October 2025. We're seven months in and retailers are already cutting deep to move units ahead of Memorial Day weekend.

That's not a commentary on the M5 Air itself - it's a solid machine, probably the best laptop value Apple makes right now. But Amazon doesn't discount brand-new MacBooks this aggressively unless they're sitting on inventory they need gone, which suggests either demand softened faster than expected or Apple's production run was too optimistic. The M4 Air barely had time to breathe before M5 showed up, and now M5 is already getting the clearance treatment. Apple's annual update cycle is creating a secondary market problem where last year's premium product feels stale by summer.

The really telling discount is the M3 iPad Air at $400 off. That's a two-generation-old device getting blown out at 32% below launch price, which means Apple and its retail partners are desperate to clear space before - presumably - an M6 iPad Air refresh this fall. The M3 Air came out in May 2024, so it's now two years old and functionally obsolete in Apple's eyes even though it still handles 90% of what most people actually do on an iPad.

Meanwhile, the iPhone 17 Pro Max is getting a rare $99 markdown barely eight months after launch. Apple's iPhone pricing has always been sticky, so when you see movement this early in the cycle it usually means carrier subsidies are drying up and retailers are testing whether consumers will bite without the monthly payment massage. They will, obviously, because $99 off a $1,299 phone still leaves you at $1,200, but the psychology matters.

The subtext here is that Apple's faster hardware cadence is training customers to wait for discounts that arrive sooner every year, which is exactly the opposite of what premium pricing is supposed to accomplish.
