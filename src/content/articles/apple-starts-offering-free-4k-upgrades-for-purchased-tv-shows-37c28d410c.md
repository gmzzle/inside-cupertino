[9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/07/06/apple-starts-offering-free-4k-upgrades-for-purchased-tv-shows/) reported that Apple is now offering free 4K upgrades for TV shows you've already purchased, something it's been doing for movies since 2017. That's a nine-year gap for what should've been the same policy from day one.

The original logic made sense: when Apple launched the Apple TV 4K in 2017, it upgraded your HD movie purchases to 4K automatically. No double-dipping, no "buy the remastered edition" nonsense like you'd see elsewhere. It was a surprisingly consumer-friendly move that made iTunes purchases feel less like a rental with extra steps. But TV shows got left out, presumably because of how licensing works—shows involve more rights holders, more seasons, more complications. Still, nearly a decade is a long time to wait for parity.

This matters more than it sounds like it should. People who bought TV show seasons on iTunes back in the 2010s—*Breaking Bad*, *Game of Thrones*, whatever—have been sitting on HD copies while Netflix and Max served up 4K streams of the same content. It's been a quiet erosion of the value proposition for owning digital content outright. Apple's pitch has always been that purchases stick with you, but only if they don't degrade relative to what's available elsewhere.

The rollout appears to be selective so far, which tracks with how this stuff works. Not every show has a 4K master, and not every studio is going to hand one over without renegotiating terms. But the precedent is set now, and that's the part that matters. If you've been holding off on buying TV shows because you figured Apple would eventually get around to this, well, you were right.

It's a good move, just embarrassingly late.
