[AppleInsider](https://appleinsider.com/articles/26/06/29/apple-design-award-winner-acquired-by-apple-for-new-swift-tools?utm_source=rss) reported that Apple acquired Rabbit 3 Times, Inc., the company behind Play, a visual development tool for Swift prototyping. This comes after Apple gave Play an Apple Design Award for innovation. The deal appears to be an asset purchase with optional staff hiring, not a full acquisition.

This tracks with how Apple operates when it finds developer tools it likes. The Swift Package Index acquisition was about keeping infrastructure running. This one looks different - more like Apple saw something useful in Play's visual prototyping approach and decided to own the tech rather than just partner with it. Whether the Play app itself continues is unclear, but the smart money says no. Apple tends to buy these tools, extract what it wants for Xcode or other first-party offerings, and let the original product sunset quietly.

The timing is interesting. Swift has been around for a decade now, and Apple keeps investing in making it more approachable. Visual tools for prototyping fit that strategy perfectly, especially if Apple wants SwiftUI adoption to keep climbing. Play's core idea - quickly mocking up app interfaces without writing boilerplate - is exactly the kind of thing that could reduce friction for new developers or speed up early-stage work for experienced ones.

The acquihire angle makes sense too. Small developer tools companies often have people who understand the pain points of the platform better than anyone inside the mothership. Apple's developer tools team has gotten better over the years, but it's still prone to shipping half-baked features and letting them languish. Bringing in people who built something developers actually wanted to use could help.

What's funny is giving a company a Design Award and then buying it a year later. It's like Apple was running an extended job interview and just decided to make an offer.
