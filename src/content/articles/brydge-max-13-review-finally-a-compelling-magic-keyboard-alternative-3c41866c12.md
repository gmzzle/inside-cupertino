[AppleInsider](https://appleinsider.com/articles/26/06/05/brydge-max-13-review-finally-a-compelling-magic-keyboard-alternative?utm_source=rss) just reviewed the Brydge Max 13, and the interesting part isn't that it exists—it's that Brydge priced it at $400, basically matching Apple's own Magic Keyboard instead of undercutting it. That's either incredibly bold or incredibly stupid, and the answer depends on whether you think the iPad Pro accessory market has room for a premium alternative that doesn't say Apple on the box.

Most Magic Keyboard competitors have gone the obvious route: plastic chassis, $150 price tag, good enough for people who just need keys and a trackpad. Brydge went the other direction. All-aluminum construction, detachable design, and a stand that works independently of the keyboard. It's heavier than Apple's option and doesn't have the floating hinge, but you can prop up your iPad without the keyboard attached, which the Magic Keyboard absolutely cannot do. If you're the kind of person who sketches with the Pencil half the day, that might actually matter.

The calculus here is whether enough iPad Pro owners are annoyed by the Magic Keyboard's limitations to pay near-identical money for something different. Apple's version is still probably better if you're basically using your iPad as a laptop replacement all day—the hinge is genuinely excellent, and the pass-through charging is cleaner. But the Brydge setup makes more sense if you're bouncing between modes. The stand-only configuration is lighter for drawing or reading, and you're not stuck with a keyboard flopping around on the back when you don't need it.

Brydge has been making iPad keyboards since before Apple cared to, and they've had some genuinely good products mixed in with some forgettable ones. This feels like the first time in a while they've shipped something aimed squarely at the same customer Apple is chasing, rather than trying to mop up budget buyers. Whether that customer exists in large enough numbers to justify the development costs is another question entirely, but at least someone's trying to compete on quality instead of just price.
