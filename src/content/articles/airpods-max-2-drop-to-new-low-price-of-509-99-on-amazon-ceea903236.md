[MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/05/03/airpods-max-2-new-low-509/) reports the AirPods Max 2 hit $509.99 on Amazon this week, down from the $549 launch price. That's a new low for headphones that shipped less than a month ago.

Here's the thing: when premium products get discounted this fast, it usually means one of two things. Either demand is softer than expected, or the retailer knows the price was too high to begin with. Apple raised the Max from $549 to $549—wait, no, they kept it at $549 but now you're getting USB-C and slightly better ANC for the same money the original cost in 2020. Inflation-adjusted, that first-gen price would be around $650 today. So technically this is a deal, but only against Apple's own anchoring.

The real problem is that $549 puts the Max 2 in direct competition with Sony's WH-1000XM6 and Bose QuietComfort Ultra, both of which regularly sell for $379-$399 and, honestly, sound just as good for most people. The Max have better build quality and deeper Apple ecosystem integration, sure. But that's a $150+ premium for aluminum cups and the ability to switch between your iPhone and Mac slightly faster. It's a tough sell.

Amazon moving volume at $510 a month after launch suggests they're sitting on more inventory than they expected. Apple probably gave them margin room to discount because they knew the price would be a barrier. It's the same pattern we saw with the Studio Display—launched high, stayed high officially, but third parties quietly cut it within weeks because nobody wanted to pay $1,599 for a monitor with an A13 chip.

If you want AirPods Max 2, $510 is legitimately the best price you'll see for a while, and they're still excellent headphones. But the discount this early is Apple accidentally admitting the market wasn't ready to pay flagship smartphone money for over-ear cans in 2026.
