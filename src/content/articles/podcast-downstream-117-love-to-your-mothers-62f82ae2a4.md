Jason Snell and Julia Alexander are ending Downstream, their streaming-industry podcast that's been running since 2021. [Six Colors](https://sixcolors.com/podcast/2026/05/downstream-117-love-to-your-mothers/) posted the final episode this week, and yeah, there's something appropriately meta about a podcast dissecting the streaming wars outlasting most of the shows it covered.

Downstream carved out a specific niche: smart people talking about the business side of streaming without the breathless hype or the doom-spiraling. That meant covering Apple TV+ launches, HBO Max rebrands, Disney+ price hikes, and the part where every service decided password-sharing was suddenly unforgivable. The show ran 117 episodes, which is more than Severance has managed in twice the time.

Snell's been writing about Apple for decades, but Downstream wasn't an Apple podcast—it was about the industry Apple decided to wade into with TV+. That context mattered. When Apple spent billions on shows nobody watched, Downstream could explain why Apple was fine with that in ways an entertainment-only podcast couldn't. When Ted Lasso ended and Apple had no obvious replacement, they talked about it like the strategic problem it was, not just a bummer for fans.

The timing makes sense. The streaming wars have cooled into a stalemate. Most services have raised prices, cracked down on sharing, and stopped pretending they'll all coexist peacefully. The gold-rush chaos that made for good podcast fodder has settled into a boring oligopoly. Apple TV+ is still around, still spending, still not really mattering outside its handful of prestige hits. There's less to say every week.

Downstream was good, and now it's done. Not every podcast needs to run forever, even when the topic still exists.
