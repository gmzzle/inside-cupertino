According to [9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/07/23/two-new-macs-might-skip-m5-chips-and-go-straight-to-m6/), Mark Gurman's latest roadmap suggests two new Mac models will skip the M5 generation entirely and jump straight to M6. Which is strange, because Apple's chip naming convention has been pretty straightforward since the M1 launched: every Mac gets the current generation, then waits for the next one.

The most likely explanation is that these two models—probably lower-volume machines like the Mac Studio or Mac Pro—simply aren't getting refreshes on the M5 cycle. Apple's been doing annual chip updates for MacBook Air and Pro, but the high-end desktop Macs have always been on a slower cadence. The Mac Studio went from M1 Max/Ultra in 2022 to M2 Max/Ultra in 2023, then… nothing for M3. If it doesn't get an M5 refresh and shows up again with M6 in 2026 or 2027, that's less "skipping a generation" and more "Apple doesn't update this thing every year."

The weirder interpretation is that Apple's planning some kind of architectural split. Maybe M6 represents a bigger leap—new process node, new core design—and Apple doesn't want to waste engineering resources on an M5 variant for low-volume Macs. That would explain why these models are skipping ahead, but it would also mean M5 is a half-step generation for the volume products. Which would be fine, except Apple doesn't usually telegraph that kind of thing.

Either way, the subtext here is that Apple's chip release cadence is getting messier. The M1 generation was clean: everything got updated within about 18 months. M2 stretched that timeline. M3 left the Mac Studio and Mac Pro behind entirely. Now M4 is rolling out in fits and starts, and apparently M5 won't even touch some Macs. The naming convention still sounds tidy, but the actual product cycles are starting to look like the Intel years, where half the lineup was always a generation behind.

If you're shopping for a high-end Mac, the lesson is the same as it's always been: buy it when you need it, because the update schedule is vibes-based at this point.
