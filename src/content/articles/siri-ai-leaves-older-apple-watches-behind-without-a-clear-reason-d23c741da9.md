Apple finally addressed why Siri AI in watchOS 27 only works on Apple Watch Series 9 and newer, and the answer is basically no answer at all. In an interview with [AppleInsider](https://appleinsider.com/articles/26/06/19/siri-ai-leaves-older-apple-watches-behind-without-a-clear-reason?utm_source=rss), Apple's Cait Dooley said the features "work best on newer hardware." That's it. That's the explanation.

Here's what makes this particularly strange: Siri AI on Apple Watch requires a nearby iPhone that supports Apple Intelligence. The watch isn't processing the AI queries itself - your iPhone 16 or later is doing the actual work. So the Series 9 cutoff can't be about processing power or on-device capability in any meaningful way. The Series 8, which came out just two years ago and shares the same S8 chip as the Ultra, apparently can't relay queries to a phone that's perfectly capable of handling them.

Apple has form here. They've cut off devices before based on chipsets, RAM, or Neural Engine requirements. Those cutoffs made sense because the device itself had to run the feature. This is different. The watch is essentially acting as a microphone and display for intelligence that lives on your phone. A Series 8 can already handle Siri requests, stream audio, and show notifications from your iPhone. What additional hardware requirement could Siri AI possibly have that existing Siri doesn't?

The most likely explanation is that Apple wants a clean marketing line and doesn't want to deal with edge cases where older watches might struggle with new interaction patterns or UI elements. Fair enough - but then just say that. Claiming it's about hardware when the hardware in question isn't doing the compute feels like the kind of non-answer that frustrates customers who just spent $400 on a Series 8 last year.

The Series 9 line isn't arbitrary in Apple's product strategy, but in this specific case, it sure looks like it.
