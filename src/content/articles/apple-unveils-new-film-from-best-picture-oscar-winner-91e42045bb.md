[9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/07/20/apple-unveils-new-film-from-best-picture-oscar-winner/) reported that Sian Heder's next film, *Being Heumann*, now has a release date on Apple TV+. This is the director's first project since *CODA* became the first streaming film to win Best Picture in 2022, which also happened to be Apple's first Oscar in that category. The company paid $25 million for *CODA* at Sundance in 2021. That bet paid off in cultural capital, even if the movie's actual viewership numbers remain a mystery Apple will never share.

*Being Heumann* is a biopic about Judy Heumann, the disability rights activist who helped draft the Americans with Disabilities Act and served in the Clinton and Obama administrations. It's a natural follow-up for Heder, whose *CODA* centered a deaf family and won praise for its casting and authenticity. Apple clearly wants to repeat that formula: hire a director with credibility on representation, pick a story with built-in awards appeal, and hope the Academy notices again. The playbook isn't subtle, but it worked once.

The risk here is that Apple TV+ still doesn't have a clear identity beyond "the place that sometimes wins Oscars." *Ted Lasso* ended. *Severance* is great but hardly a cultural juggernaut. *The Morning Show* exists. The service has 25 million paying subscribers in the U.S., per estimates, which is fine but not impressive for a company with 2 billion active devices. Prestige films like *Being Heumann* generate headlines and awards buzz, but they don't move the subscriber needle the way a *Stranger Things* or *The Last of Us* does for competitors.

Heumann's story matters, and Heder is a capable director. But Apple's approach to film increasingly feels like it's optimized for Oscar night, not for building a library people actually talk about. Netflix dumps $17 billion a year into content and still struggles with this. Apple's spending a fraction of that, which makes every project feel like it has to be a statement. Sometimes that works. Sometimes you just end up with expensive movies that disappear after their awards campaign ends.

Apple hasn't announced a release date yet, but expect a limited theatrical run followed by a streaming debut timed for awards eligibility. Because of course.
