Apple's adding a contextual paste button to the iOS 27 keyboard that shows you what's in your clipboard and where it came from. [MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/07/01/ios-27-new-copy-and-paste-feature/) spotted it in the beta: copy a link in Safari, open Notes, and a "paste from Safari" button appears above the keyboard with a preview of what you copied. One tap, it's in.

This is smart for the same reason auto-filling verification codes was smart back in iOS 12. It eliminates a repetitive gesture—long-press, wait for the popup, tap paste—and replaces it with something faster and more explicit. You can see what you're about to paste before you commit, which means fewer accidental pastes of the wrong thing. The app attribution is a nice touch too. If you've copied three things in the last minute and can't remember which was which, "paste from Reddit" is clearer than a generic clipboard icon.

The bigger story here is that Apple's slowly turning the keyboard into a context-aware utility bar. It already surfaces one-time codes, autocomplete suggestions, and formatting tools depending on what you're typing. A paste shortcut that reads your clipboard and the app you just left is a natural extension of that. It's the kind of feature Android's Gboard has played with in various forms, but Apple's advantage is system-level integration. This works everywhere because it's baked into the keyboard framework, not bolted onto individual apps.

What makes this notable isn't the feature itself—it's that Apple shipped it at all. These micro-optimizations tend to get deprioritized in favor of headline features for keynote slides. But improving copy-paste, something people do dozens of times a day, has more cumulative impact than most AI gimmicks. iOS 27 isn't out until fall, but this is already one of the updates I'll actually notice.
