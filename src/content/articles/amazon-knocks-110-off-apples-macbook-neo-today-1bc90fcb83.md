[AppleInsider](https://appleinsider.com/articles/26/07/08/amazon-knocks-110-off-apples-macbook-neo-today?utm_source=rss) spotted the 512GB MacBook Neo down to $689.99 at Amazon in the Indigo colorway, $110 below Apple's new post-increase pricing. That's the lowest it's been since Apple bumped prices across the Mac and iPad lines a few weeks back.

Here's the thing: Amazon doesn't discount new Apple hardware this aggressively out of the goodness of its heart. The Neo launched in March at $799 for 512GB, which was already a stretch for what amounted to Apple's new budget laptop with an M3 chip and a polycarbonate shell. Solid machine, fine screen, perfectly usable—but it never quite caught fire the way Apple hoped. The price hike to $799 (now effectively $800 after rounding) made it even less compelling against refurb M2 Airs hovering in the same range.

So Amazon's sitting on stock, Apple's already collected its wholesale cut, and the retailer would rather move units at $689 than let them gather dust. The Indigo finish probably isn't helping—it's the least popular color by a mile, which is why you're not seeing Space Gray or Starlight at this price. This is classic channel clearance dressed up as a deal.

The Neo itself is fine if you need a new Mac under $700 and don't care about the plastic chassis or the lack of MagSafe. But the broader point is that Apple's pricing strategy on the low end is a mess right now. The Neo was supposed to slot in under the Air and capture the education and budget-conscious crowd. Instead, it's getting undercut by its own previous-gen models and discounted by third parties almost immediately. That's not a sign of demand outstripping supply.
