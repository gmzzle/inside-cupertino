[AppleInsider](https://appleinsider.com/articles/26/06/23/tvos-27-beta-code-backs-up-homepod-and-apple-tv-siri-ai-rumors?utm_source=rss) spotted references in the tvOS 27 beta that point to Apple Intelligence coming to HomePod and Apple TV. About time.

Apple's living room hardware has been stuck with the old, frustratingly literal Siri while iPhones got the full AI treatment last year. It's been an odd gap—especially for HomePod, which is supposed to be a voice-first device. Asking your phone to "play something chill" works. Asking your $299 speaker the same thing gets you a web search result. The discrepancy made HomePod feel like a legacy product in its own category.

The WWDC 2026 keynote spent serious time on Siri AI improvements but said nothing about tvOS or HomePod getting those features. Apple did toss tvOS a reworked Podcasts app and AI-generated subtitles, which is fine but not exactly tentpole stuff. The silence felt deliberate—either the features weren't ready or Apple wanted to space out announcements. Based on what developers are finding in the beta now, it looks like the latter.

Bringing Apple Intelligence to these devices makes practical sense. The HomePod mini has been a solid entry-level smart speaker, but it's been outclassed by Google and Amazon on voice smarts for a while now. Apple TV, meanwhile, has the processing headroom—especially the newer models with the A15 chip—and adding contextual Siri would make voice navigation actually useful instead of a gimmick you try once and forget. The real question is whether Apple gates this behind the newest hardware or backports it to older Apple TV 4Ks and second-gen HomePods. My guess: they'll draw the line somewhere that conveniently encourages an upgrade.

If this ships in tvOS 27's public release this fall, it'll close a weird gap in Apple's AI rollout and make the living room feel less like an afterthought.
