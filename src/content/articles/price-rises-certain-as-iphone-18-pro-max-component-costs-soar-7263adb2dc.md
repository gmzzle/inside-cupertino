[AppleInsider](https://appleinsider.com/articles/26/07/09/price-rises-certain-as-iphone-18-pro-max-component-costs-soar?utm_source=rss) is reporting that the iPhone 18 Pro Max bill of materials is running $300 higher than the iPhone 17 Pro Max. If Counterpoint Research's numbers are even close, Apple's going to pass most of that along. We're probably looking at a $200-plus bump on the Pro Max when it ships this fall.

The global chip shortage is still biting. Apple already raised prices across the board earlier this year—the first time it touched *every* model at once—but it notably left the iPhone alone. That wasn't mercy. It was timing. The iPhone 18 was already locked into higher component costs, so why eat margin on the current generation too?

This isn't the first time Apple's paid more to build an iPhone. The iPhone 15 Pro Max saw a 12% BOM increase over the 14 Pro Max. The 16 lineup ticked up slightly. But $300 is a different scale. For context, the iPhone 17 Pro Max starts at $1,199. A $200 increase would put the base model at $1,399, and the 1TB version would crack $1,799. That's iPad Pro money for a phone.

Apple's not going to absorb this. It never does. The company's margin discipline is ironclad, and it's not about to let a chip crunch erode what is still its most profitable product line. The question is whether demand holds at those prices. The Pro Max has always been the halo product for people who want the biggest screen and the best camera, but there's a ceiling. At some point, buyers either step down to the regular Pro or just wait another year.

The iPhone 18 Pro Max was already going to be expensive. Now it's going to be *really* expensive.
