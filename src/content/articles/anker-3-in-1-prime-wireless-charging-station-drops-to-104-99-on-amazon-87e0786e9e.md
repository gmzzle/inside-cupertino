Anker's Prime 3-in-1 Wireless Charging Station is down to $104.99 on Amazon this weekend, which [MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/05/24/anker-3-in-1-prime-104/) notes matches its all-time low. Normally $150, so you're saving $45. More interesting than the discount, though, is that this is one of the first third-party chargers that actually charges at full 25W MagSafe speeds thanks to Qi2.2 support.

Most Qi2 chargers top out at 15W. Apple's own MagSafe puck does 25W, but that costs $40 by itself and doesn't charge your Watch or AirPods. Anker's doing all three simultaneously here, which makes the math work if you were considering Apple's ecosystem of standalone chargers. The Qi2.2 spec is new enough that most accessory makers haven't shipped hardware yet, so Anker's essentially first to market at scale.

The build quality is solid—aluminum base, adjustable iPhone arm that tilts, no flimsy plastic feel. The Watch charger is tucked into the base rather than stuck on the side as an afterthought. AirPods go flat on the bottom. It's a clean nightstand setup if you're trying to consolidate cables, and the 25W charging speed means your iPhone 16 Pro actually hits 50% in around 30 minutes instead of the hour-plus you'd get with slower Qi2 chargers.

One caveat: you need a 45W USB-C power adapter to hit the full 25W iPhone charging speed, and Anker doesn't include one in the box. They'll happily sell you their own for another $30, of course. Budget accordingly.

At $105, this undercuts Apple's individual MagSafe accessories by a wide margin and does it faster than most of the competition.
