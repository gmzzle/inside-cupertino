[9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/07/07/pocket-casts-update-brings-three-new-features-including-carplay-chapter-artwork/) reported that Pocket Casts shipped an update with three new features, headlined by CarPlay chapter artwork support. Which is nice, except Apple Podcasts has had this since iOS 14 in 2020. Six years is a hell of a lag for a table-stakes feature.

Chapter artwork matters more than it sounds like it should. Podcasts with proper chapter markers—think ATP, Accidental Tech Podcast, or any show that bothers with production—use artwork to signal topic shifts or ad breaks. On CarPlay, where you're glancing at the screen between stoplights, that visual cue is the difference between knowing where you are in a three-hour show and just seeing a static logo. Pocket Casts users have been asking for this forever. The fact that it's landing in mid-2026 suggests the app's development pace is less "agile" and more "eventual."

The other two features are quieter. Pocket Casts added playback speed controls directly in the mini player, so you don't have to drill into the full Now Playing screen to toggle between 1x and 1.2x. Fine. Useful if you're the kind of person who shifts speeds constantly, annoying if you're not and now there's another button to accidentally tap. They also added a setting to auto-archive episodes after playback, which is just catch-up work—Overcast has had this since 2014.

Pocket Casts used to be the power-user pick, back when Apple Podcasts was a mess and Overcast was still finding its feet. Now it's in this weird middle ground: more features than Apple's app, less polish than Overcast, slower to ship than either. The CarPlay chapter artwork is a good addition. It's also a reminder that "good addition" and "should have been there years ago" can both be true.

If you're deep in the Pocket Casts ecosystem, this update is worth grabbing. If you're shopping for a podcast app in 2026, it's hard to argue this vaults Pocket Casts back to the top of the list.
