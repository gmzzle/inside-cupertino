[9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/06/28/m7-ultra-mac-studio-coming-in-2028-after-m5-ultra-model-later-this-year/) reported that Apple's Mac Studio is getting an M5 refresh later this year, followed by an M7 Ultra model in 2028 that could bring "more interesting changes." Translation: the 2024 model is a spec bump, the 2028 one might actually be new hardware.

This matters because the Mac Studio has been coasting on its 2022 design for six years by that point. The aluminum block with the fan on top works fine—thermals are good, it's quiet, whatever—but it's also the least ambitious desktop Apple's shipped in a decade. No expandability, no user-serviceable parts, just a very fast Mac mini that costs three times as much. The M5 update keeps it current, but it doesn't fix the fundamental issue: this thing should offer *something* a Mac mini doesn't beyond raw chip speed.

The M7 timing is interesting because it lines up with when Apple might actually need to rethink the product. By 2028, the gap between Ultra chips and whatever Apple's doing in the Mac Pro will be narrower—assuming the Mac Pro still exists as a distinct product and not just a Studio with PCIe slots bolted on. If the Studio is going to justify its spot in the lineup, it either needs to get cheaper, get more capable, or get a reason to exist beyond "iMac power, headless."

What would a redesign even look like? More ports seems obvious—two Thunderbolt buses instead of one, maybe front-facing ports that aren't USB-A. Internal storage expansion would be wild but Apple's never doing that. A smaller footprint using whatever cooling tricks the M7 enables would at least make it feel new. The safest bet is they add a MagSafe charging port and call it innovation.

The M5 model landing this year is fine, necessary even, but the M7 is the one that'll show whether Apple thinks the Mac Studio is a real product category or just a placeholder until they figure out what pro desktops actually need to be.
