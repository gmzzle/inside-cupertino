The MacBook Neo launched in May with a lot of questions and apparently not many buyers. [9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/07/08/deals-macbook-neo-m5-pro-macbook-pro-ipad-pro/) spotted the 512GB model at $110 off this week, which is a meaningful discount for a machine that's barely two months old. Apple doesn't do that with products that are moving.

For context, the Neo was supposed to test whether there's a market for an ultra-portable MacBook that splits the difference between Air and Pro. The answer so far appears to be no. Retailers don't discount new Apple hardware this aggressively unless they're sitting on inventory. The M3 MacBook Air took eight months to see discounts this steep. The Neo got there in eight weeks.

Meanwhile, the M5 Pro MacBook Pro with 2TB of storage is $449 off, which is actually a solid deal if you need that configuration. That's the 14-inch model with the binned M5 Pro chip, 18GB of RAM, and enough storage to avoid the "do I really need to keep these files" conversation for another year. The M5 Pro machines have been out since March and have held pricing better than the Neo, which makes sense—they're the actual workhorse laptops people buy.

The M5 iPad Pro at $250 off is less interesting because iPad Pros always see discounts like this within a few months. Apple prices them high knowing retailers will shave 15-20% off to move units. That's been the pattern since the M1 iPad Pro in 2021, and nothing about the M5 version changes the calculus. It's a great tablet with more power than anyone needs and software that still doesn't take full advantage of it.

The Neo discount is the real story here. Apple's experimental products usually get time to breathe before retailers panic. This one didn't.
