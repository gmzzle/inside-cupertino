[AppleInsider](https://appleinsider.com/articles/26/05/28/microsoft-is-killing-office-2019-for-mac-and-iphone-and-you-cant-do-much-about-it?utm_source=rss) reported that Microsoft is effectively bricking Office 2019 for Mac, iPad, and iPhone on July 13, 2026. Not ending support—actually stopping it from working. That's a different animal entirely.

Microsoft ended support for Office 2019 back in October. Fine. Most software eventually stops getting updates. But this is a remote kill switch. On July 13, your perpetual license becomes worthless, and your only options are buying Office 2024 outright or subscribing to Microsoft 365. For anyone who paid $150 for a one-time purchase expecting it to last as long as their Mac did, this stings.

The timing matters. Office 2019 launched in September 2018. It got official support until October 2025—roughly seven years. That's not terrible by industry standards, but now Microsoft is adding a hard cutoff just nine months later. There's no technical reason the app can't keep working on your Mac indefinitely. Word processors don't need cloud infrastructure to open .docx files. Microsoft just doesn't want you using software you're not paying them for monthly.

This is the subscription endgame playing out in real time. Microsoft's entire business model now revolves around recurring revenue, and perpetual licenses are a rounding error they'd rather eliminate. Apple did the same thing years ago when it stopped selling iWork and made it free with new devices—killing the old boxed software model entirely. The difference is Apple didn't flip a switch and brick the old versions.

For Mac users stuck in institutional environments where IT controls software purchases, this is especially frustrating. You can't just switch to Pages or Google Docs when your company runs on Excel macros and SharePoint integrations. Microsoft knows this. They're betting most people will grumble and subscribe rather than fight their IT department or retrain on new tools. They're probably right.
