[9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/05/21/spotify-building-ai-powered-tool-that-will-let-subscribers-make-music-covers-and-remixes/) reports Spotify is working on AI-powered remix tools that will let subscribers make their own covers and variations of existing songs. The company is also apparently still dealing with whatever icon disaster prompted users to wait for "their good old fashioned app icon" to return, but sure, let's focus on the AI remixing thing.

This feels like Spotify watched TikTok's entire music-as-raw-material culture and decided to productize it without thinking through the legal morass. User-generated remixes aren't new - SoundCloud spent years as the Wild West for bootlegs and edits before labels cracked down. But those were users uploading files. Spotify wants to build the tool itself, which means they're either sitting on licensing deals we haven't heard about yet, or they're about to have some very expensive conversations with rights holders.

The optimistic read is that Spotify negotiated this as part of broader licensing renewals and plans to share revenue from AI-generated content. The pessimistic read is that they're launching in a limited beta, seeing what sticks, and figuring out the legal framework later - the classic tech company playbook. Either way, the economics get weird fast. If I use Spotify's tool to make a slowed-down, reverb-heavy version of a Sabrina Carpenter song, who gets paid when someone streams it? The original songwriter? The label? Me? Spotify's AI vendor? All of the above?

Apple has been conspicuously quiet on AI music tools, and this might be why. They learned from the Napster era that the music industry doesn't forgive first-mover advantage if you're moving first into their money. Apple Music's pitch has always been "we pay artists more per stream" and "we're the ones who actually respect the industry." Letting subscribers mangle catalog tracks with AI doesn't exactly fit that brand.

Spotify ships features at a pace Apple never would, which sometimes means innovation and sometimes means cleaning up messes. This one has mess written all over it.
