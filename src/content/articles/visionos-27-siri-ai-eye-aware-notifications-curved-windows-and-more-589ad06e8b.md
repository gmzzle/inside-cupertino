Apple didn't spend much stage time on Vision Pro at WWDC, but [MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/06/09/visionos-27-siri-ai-eye-aware-notifications/) dug into the visionOS 27 beta and found the kind of unglamorous improvements that actually make spatial computing less annoying to use daily.

The Siri changes are the most interesting part. You can now drop a 3D Siri orb anywhere in your space and just look at it to start talking—no wake word, no hand gesture. Visual Intelligence means Siri can see what you're looking at and answer questions about it in real time. This is exactly what should have shipped with the headset in the first place. The whole point of strapping cameras to your face is that the computer can see what you see. Took them a year and a half to make that obvious connection, but here we are.

The other notable addition: eye-aware notifications. They fade in when you glance toward them and stay subtle when you're focused elsewhere. Again, this is using the hardware that's already there—the eye tracking that Apple spent so much energy perfecting—in a way that finally feels native to the platform instead of just repurposed iPad alerts floating in space.

Curved windows are nice too, I guess. They wrap around your field of view instead of sitting flat like a theater screen in front of you. It's a visual refinement that probably makes long reading sessions less fatiguing, though whether anyone's doing long reading sessions in a Vision Pro is still an open question.

None of this is revolutionary, which is sort of the point. Vision Pro launched with impressive tech and a half-baked software experience. visionOS 27 feels like Apple filling in the obvious gaps rather than chasing the next moonshot feature. Sometimes that's exactly what a platform needs.
