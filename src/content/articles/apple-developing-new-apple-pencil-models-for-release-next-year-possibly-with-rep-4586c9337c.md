[9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/07/12/apple-pencil-ipad-new-battery-replacement/) reports that Apple is prepping new Apple Pencil models for spring 2027, including refreshed versions of both the USB-C model and the Pencil Pro. The headliner: replaceable batteries, likely driven by EU rules kicking in that year requiring user-serviceable power cells in portable electronics.

This isn't Apple suddenly finding religion on right-to-repair. It's compliance. The EU's battery regulation mandates that by 2027, rechargeable batteries in devices like styluses must be removable and replaceable by the end user using commonly available tools. Apple fought similar moves for years with iPhones, and now the Pencil gets the same treatment. The current Apple Pencil lineup is fully sealed—once the lithium-ion cell degrades after a couple years of heavy use, you're buying a new $79 or $129 stylus. That's been the deal since 2015.

The timing lines up with new iPad Pro models, which makes sense. Apple doesn't refresh the Pencil on its own cadence; it ties stylus updates to the tablets that use them. The current Pencil Pro launched in May 2024 with the M4 iPad Pros, adding squeeze gestures and barrel roll detection. A 2027 update would give Apple three years on that generation, which is about right for iPad accessories. The USB-C Pencil, meanwhile, is the budget option at $79, so seeing it get a battery door feels almost generous—until you remember Apple had no choice.

The real question is how Apple implements this. A screw-off cap and a watch battery? A sliding compartment? The Pencil's thin, balanced form factor doesn't leave much room for user-access panels without compromising the design. Apple's going to complain loudly about how this ruins the elegance, just like it did with USB-C on iPhones. Then it'll ship a version that's probably fine, and in two years everyone will forget the Pencil was ever sealed in the first place.
